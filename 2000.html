Welcome to the 2000s

This webpage was created by Ross O'Neil

History

The 2000s were marked by significant global events including the September 11 attacks in 2001, the subsequent War on Terror, and the global financial crisis of 2008. It was also a decade of technological advancement with the rise of social media platforms like Facebook and YouTube.

Politics

The 2000s began with the controversial U.S. presidential election of 2000 between George W. Bush and Al Gore. The decade saw the Iraq War, the election of Barack Obama in 2008 as the first African-American president, and growing concerns about climate change.

Music

The 2000s saw the rise of pop icons like Britney Spears, Beyoncé, and Justin Timberlake. Rock bands like Linkin Park and The White Stripes dominated the charts, while hip-hop artists like Eminem and 50 Cent brought rap to mainstream audiences.

Fashion

2000s fashion was all about low-rise jeans, trucker hats, velour tracksuits, and bedazzled everything. Brands like Von Dutch, Ed Hardy, and Juicy Couture dominated the scene. The "McBling" aesthetic featured lots of pink, rhinestones, and logo mania.

Food

The 2000s were all about convenience foods and new snack obsessions. Popular items included Lunchables, Bagel Bites, and the rise of energy drinks like Red Bull. Fast food chains introduced iconic menu items like the McGriddle and the KFC Famous Bowl. Reality food shows also boomed, with "Hell’s Kitchen" and "Iron Chef" drawing huge audiences.

Sports

In the world of sports, the 2000s were packed with memorable moments. Michael Phelps made Olympic history, while athletes like Roger Federer and Serena Williams dominated tennis. The NFL saw the rise of Tom Brady and the Patriots dynasty. In football (soccer), the 2006 World Cup in Germany was a global highlight, remembered for Zidane's infamous headbutt.

Technology

The 2000s saw the rise of the iPod (2001), Facebook (2004), YouTube (2005), and the iPhone (2007). Windows XP became the dominant operating system, and flip phones like the Motorola Razr were status symbols before smartphones took over.
